Top Ten FAQ's about National Park Service!!!

Garner State Park

  • Two German immigrant families, the Magers and the Streibs, settled on land that later became Garner State Park.
  • The Magers family, German immigrants who settled in this area, opened part of their land for camping in the 1920s.
  • For thousands of years, the Frio River has enticed people to its banks. Between 10,000 and 12,000 years ago.
  • Prehistoric people camped in the area which came for the abundant game, useful plants and stone for making tools.

McKinney Falls State Park

  • Humans began occupying this area about 8,750 years ago.
  • The park officially opened to the public in 1976. Enjoy fishing, hiking, swimming, camping and more when you visit the park today.
  • Traders used the routes of McKinney for commerce.
  • Some traders may have become part of modern tribes in Texas, such as the Tonkawa.

Lost Maples State Natural Area

  • The land for Lost Maples State Natural Area was acquired by the state of Texas in 1973 and 1974, and was opened to the public in 1979.
  • Much of the area's limestone bedrock is exposed on elevated terrain, which has a shallow, discontinuous cover of dark gray stony clay (Eckrant series).
  • The 2,906-acre reserve contains 11 miles of hiking trails, 30 campsites, and eight primitive camping areas.
  • Geology - Despite a high clay content in most cases, poorly drained soils are too inextensive to be mapped.
